Game Projects

My most recent work is on Bewitched, focusing primarily on enemy AI and combat gameplay.

This graph shows a custom pathfinding graph where each sphere represents an abstract point and the color represents the cost. This is what is used to direct enemies around each other and towards the player.

Attacks create much costlier regions on the graph that other enemies avoid. Larger enemies have a higher priority and therefore attack more often and take more direct paths to the player.

Each enemy has a unique moveset that they use against the player and the player uses back against them.

Bearly Baking

Each conversation has a pre-saved set of dialogue lines with choices and rewards that play with scrolling text and voice lines.

Every day a different group of customers enters based on their friendship level and chance

Your progress with characters including events completed, conversations had, and meals cooked are all saved as the end of each day.

Bearly Baking is the most successful game I have been a part of. I acted as a systems and very basic AI programmer for this game.

Lazy Gnome Unit

This was my first group project as the sole programmer which allowed me to hone general Unity programming skills.

The camera in this game moves freely around the map, is able to rotate, and zoom in so the player is able to get to any point on the map.

Gnomes are controlled through the UI. Each platoon can be opened and gnomes selected as groups or individually.

All strategies and enemy AI are all different functions to have the squirrels attack the nuts and the gnomes attack squirrels.
